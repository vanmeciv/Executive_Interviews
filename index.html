Executive Interviews

Likert Scales

Statement 1: I am encouraged to speak my mind about the way I think things should be done in the firm.

Statement 2: I am afraid to take chances with "risky" ideas in this organization.

Statement 3: I can take chances here without fear of negative repercussions.

Statement 4: I am discouraged from experimenting with new ways of solving company problems.

Statement 5: It is fatal for me to make a mistake.

Statement 1: I get very little information about what other people are doing.

Statement 2: It is difficult for me to approach senior management with a concern.

Statement 3: I am up-to-date on what is going on in the organization.

Statement 4: In my department, we are the last to hear about anything.

Statement 5: I can go to top management with a problem and be heard.

Statement 5: I believe that senior management is "in touch" with the opinions and thinking of the people who work here.

Statement 1: I am managed by my boss too tightly.

Statement 2: I can use my personal initiative and judgment in carrying out my work.

Statement 3: I seldom get a chance to do things without my boss "holding my hand".

Statement 4: I am very closely supervised in my work.

Statement 1: Turf struggles among departments are common here.

Statement 2: At my company, the departments "rise and fall" as one; there is little territorialism here.

Statement 3: By and large, the different departments work cooperatively to an unusual degree here.

Statement 4: I believe that conflicts between departments get in the way of getting things done.
